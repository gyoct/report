ⓘ clustering metric definitions
Executed maker order clustering — are each hour's filled maker orders spread evenly, or filled in short bursts? Measured on the fill times of executed maker orders only (filled_qty > 0) within each clock-hour.
  • coverage — share of the hour the orders span (≈100% = active across the whole hour).
  • w90 (90% within) — smallest window holding 90% of the orders; a few seconds ⇒ one tight cluster.
  • cv_gap — sd ÷ mean of the gaps between orders: ≈0 perfectly even · ≈1 random (Poisson) · >1 bursty.
  • burstiness — (sd−mean)/(sd+mean) ∈ [−1,1]: −1 perfectly even · 0 random · +1 highly bursty.
  • max gap — longest silence between consecutive orders.
  • |alpv| (qty-wtd) — order-qty-weighted mean of abs(alpv) recorded when each maker trade is received (the alpha magnitude the fills were getting).
  • |alpv| p50..p80 — 50/60/70/80 percentiles of abs(alpv) over the hour's fills (p50 = median); the unweighted distribution of fill-time alpha magnitude.
High coverage together with a small w90 or high burstiness means the orders span the hour but arrive in separated bursts (amber = bursty, teal = even).
